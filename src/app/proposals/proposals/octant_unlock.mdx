Octant's V2 migration ushered a new era in funding impactful projects, removing the time pressure
previous Epochs created. This new mechanism, focusing on giving donors more control over their donation
schedule, will hopefully positively impact participation.

Nevertheless, the challenge of **impact bounce** remains - how to convert one-time participants into sustained community members? 
Membership tiers solve this by creating visible progression that makes Octant V2's voting features actually get used, 
while also improving allocation rates and accelerating V2 adoption.

---

## The Engagement Challenge: great acquisition, missed potential for retention

Looking at historical epochs and current initiatives, a clear pattern emerges:

### Epoch Allocation Rates

- **Epochs 5-6:** 47% of stakers donated (strong community engagement)
- **Epoch 7:** Campaign brought 2x users, but donation rate dropped to 27%
- **Epochs 8-10:** Stabilized at 23-25% donation rate

Your data shows that 735 users earned rewards in Epoch 10 but did not allocate them to projects.

### The 2 populations: donors and farmers
A careful analysis of the past 6 Epochs revealed that 2 populations coexists within the Octant ecosystem:
- 75% of users claimed rewards and did not allocate. Typical farming behaviour.
- 25% of users donated, sometimes multiple times, while engaging with the rest of the community.

Recent signals validate this challenge: The V2 migration cards generated organic sharing (people posted their Oracle/Deity status on Discord), 
proving status mechanics resonate with your community. However, one-time migration rewards don't create sustained engagement.


<div className="callout callout-insight">
**Pattern 1:** Octant excels at acquiring users (Epoch 7 campaign doubled participation), but **75% of stakers still don't allocate their rewards** to projects. This is impact bounce in action—massive missed opportunity to fund impactful projects.
</div>

### Whale concentration
A recurring problem in Web3 participation, whale concentration is particularly acute for Octant. In Epoch 10, 25% of users donates but this only
amounted to 7.6% of total rewards. We have multiple small donors and a few whales who concentrate the rewards and don't give. 


<div className="callout callout-insight">
**Pattern 2:** A few whales absorb most of the rewards. Properly incentivizing them to donate is critical to demultiply Octant's impact on funding
great projects.
</div>

## Root Cause: No Social Infrastructure for Dynamic Participation

Currently, there's no visible difference between a voter who researches projects daily and updates his allocations
and a voter who allocates once and never returns. 

Everyone is an anonymous wallet. V2 has created the **infrastructure for dynamic funding**, but there's no **social infrastructure for dynamic participation**. 
In that respect, one-time engagement is the rational choice, because there's no visible benefit to sustained involvement.

---

## The Solution: Membership Tiers That Amplify Your Initiatives

Membership tiers create visible progression that makes donating more dynamic, allocations more consistent and benefits more visible.
Tiers reward consistency in engagement, effectively amplifying your current initiatives.

### How This Amplifies Octant V2

V2's innovation allows for vote changes, but without tiers, voters allocate once and can forget about Octant. 
With tiers, a Bronze voter sees "2 more updates to get to Silver". He returns to adjust as projects ship, 
unlocks Silver status and starts getting project updates, 
then adjusts again. Virtuous circle.
As a result, projects shipping impressive work get rewarded through vote reallocations, 
creating the continuous feedback loop Octant V2 was designed for.

## Expected Impact (Conservative Estimates)

### Octant V2 Engagement

- **Current:** 238 allocators (23%), $45,919 donated (data from Epoch 10)
- **With tiers:** 320-350 allocators (32-35%), $62K-$68K donated
- **Result:** **+$64K-$88K donated annually, +$1M-1.5M in project funding **

### Reduced Whale Concentration

- **Current:** Small number of large donors dominate allocation
- **With tiers:** Platinum tier provides differentiated recognition for whales
- **Result:** High-value donors get status without needing to dominate total allocations

---

## Why This Works: Psychology of Visible Progression

**After tiers:**

- **Donate:** Lose rewards but get status + access + influence (tangible)
- **Claim:** Lose rewards and stay Bronze / nobody (visible social cost)

**The key insight:** Status, access, and recognition become tangible benefits that make donation the smart choice.
