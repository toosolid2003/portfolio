## TLDR;
Led the transformation of fragmented digital initiatives into a 
focused product strategy, resulting in $70M of projected profit.

## The problem
Fonterra, a $20B global dairy company, set a clear ambition:

Generate $50M of additional profit through digital channels in 5 years.

Howeve they had:
- no clear product strategy
- no customer understanding (no personas, journeys, or pain points)
- fragmented opportunities across markets

**Strong assets and data — but no clear path to product-driven value*.*


## My role
- Led the end-to-end product strategy phase
- Identified and prioritized high-impact opportunities
- Structured and validated product concepts with stakeholders
- Led the implementation of the first product: UX design and product decisions
- Acted as decision-maker balancing user value, business impact, and technical effort
- Manage stakeholders' consensus across the whole project


## The approach: value-first, UX second

1. Focus on where value actually exists
- Analyzed sales data to identify the most promising markets
- Prioritized China and Malaysia for deeper exploration


2. Turn data into real user understanding
- Conducted field research with customers and sales teams
- Built behavioral archetypes to model real usage patterns
- Identified gaps between current tools and actual needs


3. Translate insights into product opportunities
- Designed and iterated on multiple product concepts
- Built business cases for each concept
- Ensured each one directly supported key business goals:
  - increase high-value sales
  - improve operational efficiency
  - reduce sales workload


4. Create a scalable product roadmap
- Prioritized 7 product concepts into a coherent roadmap
- Aligned stakeholders across business and technology
- Set up a blended team (Accenture + Fonterra) to enable execution and autonomy

5. Implement the concepts
- Main UX designer on the first 2 products
- Product decision maker
- Manage multiple stakeholders across 3 geographies (APAC, LATAM and North America)


## Implementation: blend between strategic and hands-on
- Led the delivery of the first product end-to-end, mainly UX design.
- Ensured the original strategic intent translated into real product decisions
- Acted as a bridge between:
  - business stakeholders
  - product teams
  - engineering

**Focus: turn strategy into actual shipped products**

## Result: From fragmented opportunities to a $70M validated product roadmap.
- $70M projected profit over 5 years
- First product delivered with 12-month payback
- Improved operational efficiency and sales effectiveness
- Long-term partnership established between Fonterra and Accenture

## Key takeaway
This project turned a broad business ambition into a **concrete, product-driven growth engine.**

**This is typically where I step in: 
when complex systems and data exist, but haven’t yet been translated into clear, high-impact products.**
