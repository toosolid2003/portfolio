## The hassle of proving your reliability
In countries where tenants enjoy much protection, landlords tend to be extra cautious when choosing their new tenants. 
It usually translates into them asking for a mountain of documents in a bid to assess the reliability of candidates. 

In France, where I'm from, I remember being asked every possible financial document: payslips, parents' income, tax records, 
work contracts, bank extracts - even criminal records! The rental market was so competitive that if you wanted to stand a chance,
you had to comply. The stakes were high - I was looking for a place to live - and I couldn't help but feeling used by this system.

The worst part: few of this documents were actually a true reflection of my reliability. I had to disclose very private information
for someone to inaccurately assess if I could pay my rent or not. Ineffective, invasive, just plain bad. 

## Your track record is the best predictor of your reliability
This is one of these statistically-true fact that don't get translated into reality. It would have been much more accurate
to ask for a payment record instead of all this useless paperwork. The problem: there was no good way to do that. The only thing I could 
do was to provide bank extracts but I doubt any landlords will sift through 2 years worth of transactions to verify that I
paid my rent in time and in full. Especially when you had 30 applications per apartment. Enter blockchain: 
What if every rent payment generated an immutable, verifiable record—without exposing my entire financial history?

## Unfalsifiable payment records? Say no more...
That's just the core of the blockchain: immutability, provability, transparency. This is where Hestia comes: provide 
an easy way for tenants to pay their rent, have their payments recorded on-chain and, cherry on the cake, generate a verifiable,
encrypted attestation that proves all of these payments have been made on-time - with a reliability score attached. 

## Pay and prove. Radical simplicity.
The payment procedure is as simple as possible:

![Home Screen - Connect and view lease details](/images/projects/rental-dapp/Home_Screen.png)

* Connect your wallet
* Pay the rent amount
* Move on with your life

![Payment Screen - One-click rent payment](/images/projects/rental-dapp/Payment_Screen.png)

When you're looking for your next place to live, the dApp can generate an attestation that proves every payment made and attaches a reliability
score to you. Think of ratings like AAA or AA+ for tenants. Simple as that: pay and prove. The landlord can just drop the attestation ID you
gave them into a UI and all your verified payment record will appear. 





## Blockchain was meant for these use cases
I start projects with one question in mind: is this going to benefit people in real life?
This is one use case that is the product of user research - a few interviews to understand the gap between exisiting needs and exisiting
solutions - that led to a working proof-of-concept. 
Choosing Web3 technologies was easy and obvious: immutability, privacy, verifiability, all core values of the tech stack I used to build
this product. 


## Key Features
- On-chain lease agreements
- Automated rent tracking
- Behaviour-based scoring system (paying on time = higher score)
- Certified payment attestations via Ethereum Attestation Service (EAS)

## Technology
- Solidity ^0.8.28
- OpenZeppelin Contracts (Ownable, ReentrancyGuard)
- Ethereum Attestation Services for attestations
- Hardhat for testing & deployment
- ETH testnet compatible (e.g., Sepolia or Goerli)
